Chapter 2

Literature Survey & Related Work

A comprehensive review of 15 peer-reviewed papers and existing platforms that informed the design, architecture, and features of SkillSwap — from P2P learning architectures to AI-augmented peer collaboration.

Overview

The review of existing research and platforms highlights the growing importance of peer-to-peer (P2P) learning in modern education. Studies such as Help&Learn (2004) and RiPPLE (2018) demonstrate that P2P architectures and recommender systems can effectively support knowledge sharing and learner connections. Platforms like Oppia (2016) and SkillMesh (2025) show that community-based and conversational learning approaches enhance engagement and interactivity. However, most existing systems either focus on one-way teaching models or lack a true reciprocal skill-exchange mechanism.

This gap identifies the need for a platform like SkillSwap, which promotes mutual learning, personalized matching, and community collaboration — enabling users to both teach and learn in a balanced, interactive environment. The literature survey collectively underlines the potential of P2P learning platforms to democratize education and foster continuous, self-directed learning among participants.

Key Outcomes from the Survey

The literature survey reveals that peer-to-peer and collaborative learning systems have evolved significantly to enhance skill exchange, personalization, and learner engagement through technology-driven approaches. Several critical insights emerged from reviewing these 15 works:

  • Digital platforms can enable scalable and interactive peer learning through mentor-learner matching and conversational content creation
  • Semantic and algorithmic techniques such as agent-based architectures significantly improve the relevance of learning partnerships
  • Reciprocal peer recommendation systems (like RiPPLE) improve efficiency of pairing learners with complementary needs
  • AI-augmented approaches using multimodal ML show promise for predicting learning outcomes and improving intervention
  • Community-driven platforms with open-source models foster broader engagement and adoption among learners
  • Gamified and conversational formats increase time-on-platform and content retention rates

Identified Gaps

Despite the wealth of research, the reviewed studies indicate key gaps that SkillSwap was designed to address. Most research provides limited empirical evidence on long-term learning outcomes, engagement sustainability, and real-world scalability. While algorithmic and architectural frameworks are well-developed, validation through user data and controlled experimentation remains insufficient. Additionally, issues such as feedback quality, motivation consistency, and adaptive matching need deeper investigation. Most critically, no existing platform combines true reciprocal skill exchange with gamified assessment and integrated external developer profile verification in a single cohesive system — which is exactly what SkillSwap delivers.
